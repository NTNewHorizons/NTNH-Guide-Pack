# Energy

## Energy Buffer

ME networks require energy to run. The whole network shares a single energy buffer. AE devices, as well as item insertion and extraction, draw energy directly from that shared pool, while <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockVibrationChamber" showIcon="left" />, <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockEnergyAcceptor" showIcon="left" />, and <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockController" showIcon="left" /> supply energy to it. You can right-click anywhere on the network with a <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:item.ToolNetworkTool" showIcon="left" /> to inspect its energy statistics.

Because storage and distribution are handled at the network level, there is effectively no transfer-rate limit inside the network itself. Devices can draw as much energy as they need, and Energy Acceptors can insert energy as fast as the network can store it. The real limit is your total storage capacity. You can expand that shared buffer with [Energy Cells](../items-blocks/energy_cells.md).

AE2 does not internally use FE, RF, or EU. Instead, it converts those energy systems into its own unit, AE. This conversion is one-way; in GTNH, you cannot convert AE back into EU. External power can be converted to AE through <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockEnergyAcceptor" showIcon="left" /> and <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockController" showIcon="left" />, although controller faces are usually better spent on [channels](./channels.md). In addition to accepting external power, you can also use <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:item.ItemMultiPart:690" showIcon="left" /> and <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockCreativeEnergyController" showIcon="left" /> for effectively unlimited AE.

</GameScene>
Power can also be generated by <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockVibrationChamber" showIcon="left" />, but AE2 is mainly designed to work alongside tech mods with much better power generation, such as GregTech or IndustrialCraft 2.

For that reason, when you lay out your base's power infrastructure, it is usually best to think of the AE2 network as a single large power-consuming multiblock.

The conversion ratios are:

*   2 FE/RF = 1 AE
*   1 EU  = 2 AE

## Energy Storage

For obvious reasons, a network cannot accept or consume more energy in a tick than it can store. If a network can only store 800 AE, then its devices can only use at most 800 AE in that tick, and an Energy Acceptor can only insert at most 800 AE in that tick.

This is a common cause of strange behavior. For example, if you build a tiny network with only an Energy Acceptor, a Drive, a Terminal, and a few devices, then try to dump a full inventory of cobblestone into it, the network may not have enough buffered energy to handle all of that insertion in one tick. Some of the cobblestone will fail to insert, the network will run out of energy, and it may reboot.

This becomes especially obvious when using [Spatial IO](./spatial-io.md), because starting a [Spatial Pylon](../items-blocks/spatial_pylon.md) setup requires a very large amount of buffered energy. The solution is to add Energy Cells.

* Every cable, device, or part in the network provides a built-in 25 AE buffer.

* <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockController" showIcon="left" /> has a small internal buffer of 8,000 AE.

* <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockEnergyCell" showIcon="left" /> stores 200,000 AE. For most use cases, a single one is enough to absorb ordinary network power spikes.

* <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockDenseEnergyCell" showIcon="left" /> stores 1,600,000 AE. It is useful when you want to run a network from stored power or handle the very large burst consumption of big [Spatial IO](./spatial-io.md) systems.

* <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockCreativeEnergyCell" showIcon="left" /> is a late-game UHV-tier item that provides unlimited power.

* <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockCreativeEnergyController" showIcon="left" /> is the ultimate ME Controller, combining an <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockController" showIcon="left" /> and an <ItemLink id="appliedenergistics2:tile.BlockCreativeEnergyCell" showIcon="left" /> in one block.
